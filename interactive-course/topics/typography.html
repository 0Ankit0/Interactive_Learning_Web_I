Font Categories

Serif Fonts

Serif fonts have small decorative strokes called serifs. They're traditional and readable in print.

Best for: Body text, formal documents, traditional designs
Examples: Times New Roman, Georgia, Playfair Display

Sans-Serif Fonts

Sans-serif fonts lack decorative strokes. They're clean, modern, and highly readable on screens.

Best for: Headers, UI elements, modern designs
Examples: Arial, Helvetica, Inter, Roboto

Monospace Fonts

Monospace fonts have equal character widths. Every letter takes up the same horizontal space.

Best for: Code, data tables, technical content
Examples: Courier, Monaco, JetBrains Mono

Display Fonts

Display fonts are designed for large sizes and headlines. They often have unique characteristics.

Best for: Headlines, logos, decorative text
Examples: Impact, Bebas Neue, Oswald

Line Height & Spacing

Line height affects readability significantly. Too tight and text becomes hard to read. Too loose and it loses cohesion. The ideal line height for body text is typically between 1.4 and 1.6 times the font size.

Proper spacing between paragraphs, headings, and other elements creates visual breathing room and improves the overall reading experience.

The Art of Typography

Beautiful and Readable Design

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. The arrangement of type involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing, and adjusting the space between pairs of letters.

Good typography enhances the reading experience and reinforces the meaning of the text. It's not just about making text look prettyâ€”it's about making communication more effective.
