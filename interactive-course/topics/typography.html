Font Categories

Serif

Aa

Fonts with small decorative strokes (serifs) at the ends of letterforms. Often used for body text in print.

Sans-serif

Aa

Fonts without serifs, providing a clean, modern look. Widely used for web content and headings.

Monospace

Aa

Fonts where each character occupies the same amount of horizontal space. Ideal for code blocks.

Display

Aa

Decorative fonts used for headings and titles to grab attention. Not suitable for body text.

The Art of Typography

Beautiful and Readable Design

Effective font pairing is essential for creating a professional and cohesive design. The right combination of fonts can enhance readability, establish a clear hierarchy, and convey the desired tone and mood.

When pairing fonts, aim for contrast without conflict. A common strategy is to combine a serif heading font with a sans-serif body font, or vice versa. Ensure that both fonts are legible at various sizes and weights.
