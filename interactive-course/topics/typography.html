Serif Fonts

Fonts with small decorative strokes (serifs) at the ends of letterforms. Often used for body text in print and formal designs.

Aa Bb Cc Dd Ee Ff Gg

Serif fonts convey tradition, reliability, and formality. They're excellent for long-form content and printed materials.

Sans-serif Fonts

Fonts without serifs, providing a clean, modern look. Widely used for web content and headings.

Aa Bb Cc Dd Ee Ff Gg

Sans-serif fonts are highly legible on screens and work well for both headings and body text in digital interfaces.

Monospace Fonts

Fonts where each character occupies the same amount of horizontal space. Ideal for code blocks and technical content.

Aa Bb Cc Dd Ee Ff Gg

Monospace fonts are perfect for displaying code, terminal output, and any content where character alignment is important.

Display Fonts

Decorative fonts used for headings and titles to grab attention. Not suitable for body text due to poor readability.

Aa Bb Cc Dd Ee Ff Gg

Display fonts should be used sparingly and at large sizes. They're great for logos, posters, and impactful headlines.

Spacing & Scale

Proper line height (leading), letter spacing (tracking), and word spacing are crucial for readability. A modular scale ensures consistent and harmonious font sizes.

Beautiful and Readable Design

Effective font pairing is essential for creating a professional and cohesive design. The right combination of fonts can enhance readability, establish a clear hierarchy, and convey the desired tone and mood.

When pairing fonts, aim for contrast without conflict. A common strategy is to combine a serif heading font with a sans-serif body font, or vice versa. Ensure that both fonts are legible at various sizes and weights.
